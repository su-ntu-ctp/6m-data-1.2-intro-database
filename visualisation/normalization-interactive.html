Introduction

The Update Anomaly Problem

Why do we need normalization? Let's see what happens when data is stored inefficiently.

☕

Imagine a coffee shop that writes down your full home address every time you buy a coffee.

If you move house, they have to find every single coffee purchase you ever made and update your address.

This is called an Update Anomaly - the same data stored in multiple places.

Overview

The Three Normal Forms

Database normalization follows three progressive rules to eliminate redundancy.

📋
1NF
"No Lists"
Each cell contains a single value. No comma-separated lists like "Apple, Banana, Pear". Each row must be unique.
🔑
2NF
"The Whole Key"
Every column must depend on the entire primary key, not just part of it. Applies to composite keys.
🔗
3NF
"No Pass-Throughs"
No column should depend on another non-key column. Each fact depends only on the primary key.
Let's apply these rules to a real e-commerce example!
